# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fashion-curious young Chileans who don't yet know the independent/streetwear brand scene in Santiago. They're browsing, not searching with intent — the job is discovery, not transaction. They land on the globe, drag/rotate to explore, get pulled into a category, end up on a piece, and click through to the brand's own store to actually buy (PRET-A-CL is a discovery layer, not a checkout).

## Product Purpose

PRET-A-CL is a university thesis project (Título 2): a curatorial platform that aggregates pieces from ~15 independent Chilean fashion/streetwear brands (La Martic, De Goya, Ames Dept, Palo Santo, Trinidad Studio, LCS Tailors, VSA Worldwide, C. Miller, Dos Lobos, Treino, Guaico Estudio, Lalalou, Munay Sisters) into one exploratory globe-based interface. Success = the browsing/discovery mechanic itself reads as a deliberate, defensible design decision for the thesis — not just "does it convert."

## Brand Personality

Cult, editorial, austere. Voice is declarative and a little confrontational ("NO ES RETAIL. ES CULTO."), all-caps, Helvetica Neue, black on white. Confidence over warmth. No upsell language, no friendly mascot tone — it reads like a manifesto/zine, not a storefront.

## Anti-references

Do not look like generic e-commerce (Shein, default Shopify theme): no product-card grids with sale badges, no carousels, no banner promos, no rounded soft cards, no star ratings. Also avoid corporate SaaS-cream minimalism (pastel/cream backgrounds, gradient-text headlines, rounded pill buttons) — the existing black/white/sharp-corner/Helvetica system is the right direction; extend it rather than softening it.

## Design Principles

1. Discovery over search — the globe/drag mechanics are the product, not decoration on top of a product list.
2. No retail tells — every UI choice should read editorial/curatorial, never "shop now" generic commerce.
3. Monochrome with intent — black/white/Helvetica is the system; color, if ever introduced, must earn its place.
4. PRET-A-CL curates, brands sell — always route the actual purchase out to the brand's own store; never simulate a checkout.
5. Confidence in restraint — sharp corners, declarative copy, no decorative softening (no gradients, no cards-as-default, no rounded pills).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline only: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already partially implemented in index.css), keep body text contrast readable on white, ensure overlays (Lookbook, Product) are dismissable via visible close controls. No formal WCAG audit required for this thesis deliverable.
